Dupax del Norte, formally the Municipality of Dupax del Norte, is a third-class municipality in the Philippines' province of Nueva Vizcaya. It has a population of 33,295 people, according to the census of 2020. Dupax del Norte has a total land area of 396 square kilometers and is located in Nueva Vizcaya's south-eastern region. It is bordered on the north by Kasibu, on the east by Alfonso Castaeda, on the south by Dupax del Sur, and on the west by Bambang. It has vast swaths of virgin forest, fertile plains and valleys with mineral resources, and a climate conducive to farming.

HISTORY

Dupax is derived from the Isinay word "Dopaj," which means "to lie down completely relaxed." The area that finally became the "poblacion" was formerly a roaring camp for primitive hunters from the neighboring tribal communities. Dupax was the name given to the town by the Spaniards. RA 6372, sponsored by Congressman Benjamin B. Perez and Senator Leonardo B. Perez, was signed into law in 1971, establishing the Municipality of Dupax del Norte. To put the law into effect, President Ferdinand E. Marcos issued Presidential Decree No. P.D. On November 6, 1974, the town of Dupax was divided into two sections: Dupax del Norte and Dupax del Sur, therefore paving the way for the creation of Dupax del Norte and Dupax del Sur. It might be argued that Dupax's separation has some historical roots. The Malaats and the Caraos were claimed to be living in the Municipality of Dupax as early as 1725. The Malaats, commanded by Chieftain Dayag, controlled the Parai region in Dupax del Norte, while the Caraos, led by Chieftain Tiyum Pising, shared Dupax del Sur with the Isinays.
