DAMPOL BRIDGE

It is claimed to be the oldest bridge in Northern Luzon and one of the best maintained Spanish-era bridges in the Philippines. This roughly two-hundred-year-old single-arch unreinforced bridge spans the Abanatan Creek, which separates Barangay Dopaj and Domang was constructed in 1818 by the Isinai and other local indigenous communities. Its red-colored bricks were created in a nearby adobe workshop by Spanish Dominican friar Fr. Francisco Rocamora.
